Design Brief
Population explosion globally has directly resulted in urban sprawl of the respective metro cities to accommodate the ever-increasing masses mainly resulting in unsustainable living solutions. The scarcity of available land for development and an increase in real estate prices has led to the trend of utilizing mixed-use developments to solve the multitude of issues currently presented in twenty-first-century cities. This invites an exploration of the potential and opportunities these types of developments can provide in relation to their placed context. This project, therefore, will investigate the process of typology analysis in the context of the city as the first principle aid for the production of mixed-use architecture.

Vertically mixed-used developments are a system of decentralization of amenities, such that uses and facilities are available in smaller and localized areas. Activation of street edge through ground floor retail and commercial. upper-story residential for improved privacy, access to views, adequate light and ventilation, increased demand for local needs, and easy access to regional employment opportunities Multiple stakeholders in one building use resources like energy, trash disposal, and sewage more efficiently. Mixed-use highrise building is a more unified mixed and architectural solution, with certain different paradigms rather than earmarking separate buildings for different land uses.
Design Philosophy
  • To design a mixed-use skyscraper which contributes towards sustainable environment along with well-organized different sectors of the living needs.
  • To configure spaces which improve the social and visual collaboration and decrease disengagement.
  • To design a structurally full proof building to cater to the context and need optimally.
